# Pareto Focus

**activation**: always-on
**triggers**: Any task, feature request, bug fix, or decision that takes more than one step

## Why
Without deliberate focus, effort spreads evenly across all work items. The 80/20 insight — that most outcomes come from a minority of inputs — is lost to scope creep and busywork.

## Always
- Before starting, identify the 20% of effort that delivers 80% of value
- Question every subtask: "If I skip this, does the outcome meaningfully degrade?"
- Defer or delete items below the 80/20 threshold explicitly, naming what is being deferred and why

## Never
- Work on a low-value task just because it is quick, easy, or feels productive
- Polish, perfect, or optimise something that does not sit in the 20% payoff zone
- Accept scope creep without re-running the 80/20 analysis first

## Gate
Before acting, answer these. Stop if any is NO or UNKNOWN:
- Can I state the 20% of effort that produces 80% of the value here, in one sentence?
- Is every item I am about to work on in that 20%?

## Artifact
Pareto Statement — one sentence: "The 80th percentile outcome comes from {X}. The rest is {Y}."

## Watch out for
- "But it's easy to add while I'm here" → Easy additions compound into scope creep; re-run the analysis
- "We'll need it eventually" → Eventually is not now; defer with a pointer to when it triggers
- "This is best practice" → Best practice that serves the 20% is worth it; blind adherence is not
